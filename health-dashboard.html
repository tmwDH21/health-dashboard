Suboxone — Special Instructions

Suboxone must be taken sublingually (under the tongue). The naloxone component is inactive when absorbed this way — if swallowed, it partially blocks buprenorphine's effects. Dry mouth is required for proper absorption: nothing to eat or drink for 15 minutes before. Let the film or tablet dissolve completely (5–10 minutes). Do not talk, swallow, or rinse during dissolution. For best consistency, take it before your morning medications and breakfast, then proceed with the rest of your morning stack once dissolved.

⚠️ Additional Drug Interactions to Discuss With Your Doctors

Eliquis + Aspirin = Elevated Bleeding Risk
Dual therapy (anticoagulant + antiplatelet) significantly increases bleeding risk. Your platelets at 161 K/UL (low-normal) compound this. The combination is sometimes clinically indicated post-stent, but the calculus changes over time. If you're more than 1 year post-stent, discuss with your cardiologist whether aspirin is still necessary.
Suboxone + Xanax = CNS Depression Risk
Both are CNS depressants. The FDA has issued black-box warnings about combining buprenorphine with benzodiazepines. At your doses (4mg Suboxone, 1-1.5mg Xanax PRN), the risk is present but managed with careful use. Your prescribers should be in communication and aware of this combination.
Prednisone + Blood Pressure Meds = Fluid Retention
Prednisone causes sodium and water retention, which directly counteracts Valsartan and Metoprolol's blood pressure control. As you taper off prednisone, your blood pressure medications may need adjustment — don't assume BP remains stable through the taper without monitoring.
Grapefruit — Avoid Entirely
Grapefruit and grapefruit juice inhibit CYP3A4 enzymes — the same pathway that metabolizes diltiazem, atorvastatin, and alprazolam (Xanax). Even one glass can raise blood levels of these drugs unpredictably.

🟠 Priority: Cardiovascular

Exercise: zone 2 cardio is your cardiac medicine
You already exercise 2–5x/week — excellent. For your cardiac profile (stent, AFib, hypertension), Zone 2 cardio (conversational pace — 60–70% max HR) for 150–200 minutes per week is the most evidence-backed intervention. Zone 2 specifically improves mitochondrial function, insulin sensitivity, and reduces cardiac events in post-stent patients. Walking briskly, cycling, swimming — not sprint intervals. Max HR for you at 57: roughly 163 BPM; Zone 2 target: 100–115 BPM.
Get LDL and HDL numbers — today
Your atorvastatin 40mg dose should be achieving LDL <70 mg/dL (the target for very high CV risk). You don't actually know if it's working because the numeric values are locked in graph images. Call your doctor's office and ask for a printed lipid panel with LDL, HDL, and triglycerides. If LDL isn't at target, dose escalation or addition of a PCSK9 inhibitor may be warranted.
Omega-3 fatty acids — consider adding
Fish oil (EPA+DHA, 2–4 grams/day) has strong evidence for triglyceride reduction and modest cardiac protection. Prescription Vascepa (icosapentaenoic acid only) reduced cardiovascular events by 25% in the REDUCE-IT trial in patients already on statins with high triglycerides. Given your cardiovascular profile, discuss with your cardiologist. Note: at high doses, omega-3 can potentiate Eliquis — clinical supervision matters here.
Stop smoking (you have) — now protect the gains
Nicotine pouches still deliver nicotine, which is vasoconstrictive and raises blood pressure — directly relevant to your hypertension, AFib, and coronary artery disease. The goal should be complete nicotine cessation. Varenicline (Chantix) or cytisine have good evidence. The difference between smoking and nicotine pouches is that you've eliminated most of the carcinogens — but the cardiovascular effects of nicotine itself persist.

🔵 Priority: Sleep & Recovery

Prednisone causes insomnia, especially if taken at the wrong time (not morning). Morning dosing helps. AFib, hypertension, and excess weight are the three primary risk factors for obstructive sleep apnea. Sleep apnea dramatically worsens AFib and cardiovascular outcomes. If you haven't had a sleep study (or home sleep test), this is medically indicated. Treatment of sleep apnea reduces AFib burden and improves blood pressure. Home sleep test is simple and often covered.

Your daily meditation practice has measurable cardiac effects: reduced sympathetic nervous system activation, lower resting heart rate, reduced cortisol (which directly affects your glucose and immune function).
